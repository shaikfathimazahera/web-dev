The Moon

The Moon is an astronomical body orbiting Earth and is the planet's only natural satellite. It is the fifth-largest satellite in the Solar System, and by far the largest among planetary satellites relative to the size of the planet that it orbits. The Moon is, after Jupiter's satellite Io, the second-densest satellite in the Solar System among those whose densities are known. The Moon is thought to have formed about 4.51 billion years ago, not long after Earth. The most widely accepted explanation is that the Moon formed from the debris left over after a giant impact between Earth and a hypothetical Mars-sized body called Theia. New research of Moon rocks, although not rejecting the Theia hypothesis, suggests that the Moon may be older than previously thought.


More Information

Diameter

The diameter of the Moon is approximately 3,474 kilometers (about 2,158 miles). It's fascinating to think that our Moon is the fifth largest moon in the entire solar system! 🌕

Temperature

The Moon's temperature varies from 🌞 250°F (120°C) during the day to ❄️ -208°F (-130°C) at night, with polar areas reaching 🧊 -424°F.

Distance

The Moon averages 238,855 miles from Earth. 🌎🌕 At apogee, it’s 252,088 miles away, and at perigee, it’s 226,000 miles. Supermoons appear 17% larger! 🌕🔭

Velocity

The speed of light (c) is 299,792,458 meters per second (300,000 km/s or 186,000 miles/s). It’s the limit for all electromagnetic radiation in space. 🌠🚀

Phases Of Moon

Phases of Moon

Phase Name Img Description
New Moon During this phase, the Moon is positioned between Earth and the Sun. It appears completely dark from our perspective.
Waxing Crescent A thin crescent of light emerges on the right side of the Moon. It gradually grows larger as it moves toward the first quarter.
First Quarter Half of the Moon’s visible side is illuminated, creating a half-moon shape. This phase marks the midpoint between the new moon and the full moon.
Waxing Gibbous More than half of the Moon’s visible side is lit, leading to a waxing gibbous phase.
Full Moon The entire visible side of the Moon is brilliantly illuminated. It occurs when the Earth, Moon, and Sun align in a straight line.
Waning Gibbous After the full moon, the illuminated portion gradually decreases, forming a waning gibbous phase.
Third Quarter Another half-moon shape, but this time the opposite side is illuminated. It marks the midpoint between the full moon and the new moon.
Waning Crescent A thin crescent of light appears on the left side of the Moon, just before the new moon.
